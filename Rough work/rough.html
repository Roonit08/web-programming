Culture

Living heritage and festivals

The Kathmandu Valley preserves exquisite Newar craftsmanship, while monasteries and temples across Nepal celebrate festivals like Indra Jatra, Lhosar and Chhath. Architecture, art and daily life mingle in every alley.Nepal is a country rich in festivals and culture, where traditions are deeply rooted in history and celebrated with great enthusiasm. Major festivals like Dashain, Tihar, Holi, and Indra Jatra bring together people of all communities in joy and unity. These festivals often blend Hindu and Buddhist traditions, reflecting Nepal’s cultural harmony. The history of Nepal’s culture is closely tied to its ancient kingdoms, each leaving behind unique art, music, and architecture. The Durbar Squares of Kathmandu, Patan, and Bhaktapur are prime examples of this heritage, showcasing magnificent palaces, temples, and courtyards built during the Malla period. These squares are UNESCO World Heritage Sites and stand as symbols of Nepal’s architectural brilliance. They host vibrant festivals like Kumari Jatra, where the living goddess is paraded through the streets. The intricate wood carvings and pagoda-style temples in Durbar Square reveal the craftsmanship of centuries past. Visitors can witness both the sacred rituals and everyday life blending seamlessly in these historic spaces. Together, Nepal’s festivals and the grand buildings of Durbar Square create a living museum of culture, history, and tradition.Thus, the festivals and Durbar Squares of Nepal together preserve and showcase the nation’s timeless cultural and historical identity.
